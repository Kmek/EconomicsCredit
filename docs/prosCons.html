A good way to build your credit score if you have little or no credit history, but only if you pay back your outstanding balance.

Credit cards are more secure than cash. If the card is stolen or lost, the card can be locked. Banks can also monitor activity to notify you if something seems suspicious.

Credit cards can come with extra benefits and rewards when you use them regularly. Rewards differ by credit card company, but some examples are getting cash back or getting airline miles.

When used responsibly, credit cards allow users to pay for expensive purchases over time, with added interest of course.

The interest rates and other fees on credit cards can be very high. This can make you pay more extra costs than the cost of the actual purchase, when using a credit card.

If you're not careful, you can easily wind up in lots of debt due to overspending.

Too many cards can damage your credit score. If you apply for too many cards, all those lenders are hard checking your credit score which can lower your credit score overall. Companies might also be concerned about how many cards you have open, which could impact your applications for new credit cards.
